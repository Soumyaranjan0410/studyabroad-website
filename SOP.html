Statement of Purpose

Home / Documents

How to write a Personal Statement: A personal statement is your chance to tell the University why you are applying for this programme, and they should want you as a student. The admission officers will want to know why you are interested in the programme you have applied for and what you hope to do after your studies. A good personal statement is important and should help to persuade an admission officer to office you a place. In many cases, applicants are not interviewed, so this may be your only chance to make a case for you admission. There is no set format for a personal statement. Consider carefully the information you give to support your application and the best way to present it effectively. Remember, you must be truthful and accurate in what you write. It is up to you how you write your statement but we suggest you include some or all of the following points.

  • Why you have chosen the pragramme(s) you have listed
  • What interests you about you chose subject? Include details of what you have read about the subject.
  • Why do you want to study in London?
  • What career plans you have when you complete your course.
  • Any job, work experience, placement or voluntary work you have done, or applied for particularly, it is relevant to your subject. You may want to describe the skill and experience you have gained form these activities
  • Any subject studied for which you do not have a formal assessment.
  • Your social, sport or leisure interests.

    • If the space provided is insufficient, you can continue on extra pages, if you feel it is necessary. Just ensure you statement is precise and clear. If you wish to send more information, a CV is a good way to submit this. Please attach this to your application form if you feel it is appropriate.
