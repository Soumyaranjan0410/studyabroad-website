How to Write 'Reference'

Who should write the reference? The person who writes the reference for you should know you well enough to write about your and judge if you are suitable for higher education. We will not accept reference from family, other relatives or friends. You should have full written reference: the name and address of the referee are not enough if are at school or college, or have left recently, should ask your head teacher. Principal or an appropriate teacher or tutor to write this.

If you are a mature student you should a responsible person who knows you. This could be an employer, training officer, career officer, a teacher on a recent relevant further education course, or a senior colleague in employment voluntary work.

Guidance for referees: We will appropriate if you could use you letter headed paper. Please give an assessment of the suitability of the applicant for the higher education programme(s) they have applied for and where possible, include the following points.

  • Existing achievement, with particular reference to subject related to the programme(s) for which they have applied.
  • Potential, including predicted results or performance.
  • Motivation and commitment towards the chosen course or courses.
  • Any relevant skill achievement, whether certified or no.
  • Powers of analysis and independent thought.
  • Any factors which may have affected, or will affect on a future, their performance, for example personal circumstances, performance under pressure etc.
  • Information about any special needs and other requirements.
