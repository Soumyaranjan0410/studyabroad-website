IELTS – General Overview

Let’s begin with a General Overview of the IELTS exam right from what I-E-L-T-S stands for. Well, this stands for the International English Language Testing System. As the name suggests, it is a proficiency Test to measure a candidate’s ability of using the English Language. This is used by Educational institutions, employers and also the immigration people to comprehend a candidate’s ability of conversing effectively in English.


IELTS is used throughout the world as one of the most trusted testing methods. In fact it is accepted by most nations and educational organizations than any other test. It is available in two formats. The first one which is The Academic IELTS, as the name rightly suggests is required for entry to Academic institutions. The score required is slightly lower for lower levels of study like undergraduate levels than for postgraduate education. However, this is again decided by every college or university individually. The other format is given the name General Training. This is used and attempted by professionals for employment or immigration purpose. Some employers or job categories may expect you to take the Academic type of the exam. Therefore it is a good idea to confirm your individual requirement and accepted version before you start training. As you proceed, you will look at the similarities and differences between both types of exams. The test is designed and owned by the British Council, IDP Education Australia and Cambridge English Language Assessment. Therefore it has a far and wide reach with a variety of speakers, from different parts of the world. This is a special feature you may not be able to find in many other English Language Exams.

Duration of exams:

The total duration of the written tests is 2 hours and 45 minutes made up of Listening, Reading and Writing papers. These take place one after the other with no breaks in between. The Speaking test is a further 11-14 minutes. This may take place on the same day as your written test or during the week before or after your written tests. It is a one on one interview with an IELTS examiner.

Exam locations and registration:

The IELTS test is conducted throughout the world in more than 1200 locations at least 2-3 times a month. It is conducted all year round so you need not worry about only being able to appear for it at a particular time in the year. However, you must plan when you want to take the test in advance as most test centers expect you to register at least 2-3 weeks in advance due to limited number of seats. It is always a good idea to set a target date, registering for it and start preparing for the test keeping that in mind. This would certainly give you a more focused approach and help you prepare better.


The fee varies from one location to another but it would averagely cost you about $200. The proof of identification you must provide while registering for the test is your valid passport. This also has to be carried for the written papers as well as Speaking exams.
